Module 1
What is SPIN Selling?
Foundation

SPIN.
THE METHOD THAT WORKS.

You're not here to push a product. You're here to have a real conversation - and let the other person talk themselves into joining. That's what SPIN is. It's how the best salespeople operate, and it is what you are going to learn.

Why typed sales is different

On LinkedIn, Snapchat, WhatsApp, TikTok DMs - you cannot use your voice or body language. All you have got is words on a screen. That means every message has to work harder. No long paragraphs. No essays. Ask smart questions, listen to what they say, then reply to exactly what they told you.

Cold leads vs inbound leads

Cold lead - someone who has never heard of Inspiring Me. You reached out to them first. You need to build the conversation from scratch.

Inbound lead - someone who saw a post, clicked a link, or messaged you first. They already know something is going on. You still run SPIN - but you can skip the very first opener and go straight into finding out what they already know and what they are looking for. The gap still needs surfacing. Do not assume interest means they are ready to sign up.

The Framework
S
Situation
Find out where they are at right now. School, future plans, what they are working towards.
P
Problem
Surface the gap. No experience, no CV, no idea how to stand out. Let them say it.
I
Implication
Make the gap feel real. What happens if they do not fix it? What are they actually risking?
N
Need-Payoff
Let them describe the ideal solution. Then show that is exactly what Inspiring Me offers.

The golden rule

Never pitch until the gap is surfaced. This applies to both cold and inbound leads. Someone replying to your post is interested - but interested does not mean they know what they need yet. SPIN earns you the right to pitch, every time.

Message rules - always

  • Keep it under 3 lines. If it is longer, cut it.
  • One question per message. Two questions = no answer.
  • No jargon, no corporate speak. Talk like yourself.
  • End every message with an easy question or a soft next step.
  • Don't pitch the programme until they have told you what they need.
  • Acknowledge what they say before you move on.

KNOW WHAT
YOU'RE SELLING.

Before you can sell it, you need to know it inside out. Here's everything you need about Inspiring Me CIC.

What is Inspiring Me CIC?

A work experience programme for young people aged young adults across the UK. Participants get hands-on experience in field sales, fundraising, and communications - building real skills that actually show up on a CV and get recruiters' attention.

It runs on Saturdays and Sundays. Sessions are in-person across London, with personal development delivered online each week.

What do they actually do?

  • Sell the Inspiring Me magazine across London - outside busy stations, town centres, and events. All sales count.
  • Complete an online self-development module each week and update their LinkedIn profile.
  • Build a professional LinkedIn presence that actually gets noticed.
  • Graduate with a certified qualification - real proof on their CV.

What's in it for them?

  • Real CV experience - actual field sales results, not just "assisted with admin."
  • A professional LinkedIn profile built and live by the end.
  • A certificate they can point to in interviews.
  • Skills: cold approach, objection handling, closing, communication - all transferable.
  • They earn as they go - based on what they do.
On pay: Mention it once max - never stress it, never lead with it. The skills and CV value is the headline. If they ask directly: "yes, it is a programme where you earn based on what you put in." Then move on.

Fast-track pay

Complete the self-development task early → get paid on Monday instead of Friday. That's the incentive to stay on top of the online side.

S - SITUATION.
UNDERSTAND THEM FIRST.

Situation questions build the picture. You're not interrogating - you are showing genuine interest. Find out where they are at before anything else.

What you are trying to learn

  • Are they in school, college, or working?
  • What are they trying to do next - uni, apprenticeship, job?
  • Have they got any work experience already?
  • What kind of careers are they thinking about?
Cold lead example - Snapchat DM
Snapchat
Hey! Saw you go to Harris Academy - hope thats ok. Are you in sixth form or have you just finished?
S - cold opener, one question
Yeah Year 12 lol. Why?
No stress - just curious what you are thinking of doing after. Uni or work or still figuring it out?
S - follow-up, relaxed tone for Snapchat
Probably uni, maybe property or something. Not fully sure yet

What just happened?

Two short messages. You know their year, their rough direction, and they are still talking. You did not mention the programme once - and that is exactly right. On Snapchat the tone needs to be even more relaxed than LinkedIn. Match where they are.

Inbound lead example - WhatsApp
WhatsApp
Hey I saw your post about the programme - sounds interesting
Hey! Glad it caught your eye. What made you reach out - are you looking for experience or more the earning side or just curious?
S - inbound opener, find out what they already know
Mainly experience tbh I need something for my CV
Ok nice - are you still in school/college or have you finished?
S - follow-up situation question

Inbound is not the same as ready

They messaged you first - great. But you still do not know their full situation. Run the S questions to find out where they are at before you say anything about what the programme involves. The only difference from cold is you skip the very first opener.

Good vs bad situation questions

  • Good: "Are you in sixth form or college?" - specific, easy to answer
  • Good: "What are you thinking of doing after school?" - open, not pushy
  • Bad: "Are you looking for work experience?" - you have jumped ahead
  • Bad: "Would you be interested in a paid opportunity?" - pitched before earning it

P - PROBLEM.
FIND THE GAP.

Problem questions surface what they are struggling with. The gap between where they are and where they want to be. You do not create this - it is already there. Help them say it out loud.

Common gaps for young people

  • No work experience on their CV - everyone asks for it, nobody gives it.
  • Don't know how to stand out when applying for jobs or uni.
  • LinkedIn profile is either empty or barely there.
  • Want to go into business, sales, or comms - but have nothing to show for it yet.
  • Tried to get work experience but could not find anything real or relevant.
Cold lead example - LinkedIn
LinkedIn
Probably uni, maybe property or estate management. Not fully sure yet though
Makes sense - have you managed to get any experience yet in that area, or is that still something you are trying to figure out?
P - surfacing the problem
Not really no. Its hard to find anything thats actually useful, most things are just shadowing or unpaid
Yeah that is really common honestly. Is that something that feels like it is holding you back at all?
P - deepening the problem

Key move

They said it themselves - hard to find anything actually useful. You did not put those words in their mouth. When the problem comes from them it is real. That is the foundation everything else sits on.

Inbound lead example - TikTok DM
TikTok DMs
Mainly experience tbh I need something for my CV
Got it - what kind of stuff have you tried so far? Like work experience, part time jobs, anything?
P - find out what they have already tried
Nothing really lol I keep applying but never hear back
That is frustrating. Is it that there is nothing out there or more that the stuff you find is not really relevant?
P - narrowing down the specific problem
Both tbh. Like I want something that actually helps me not just like making tea

Inbound problem questions

They already told you they need CV experience - but that is vague. Problem questions help you get specific. What have they tried? What did not work? The more specific the problem the better you can connect the programme to it later.

Practice
They've just told you they are finishing Year 13 and want to go into civil engineering. They mentioned struggling to find relevant work experience.
What's the best Problem question to follow up with?

I - IMPLICATION.
MAKE IT REAL.

Implication questions make the problem feel urgent. Not by scaring them - by helping them think through what not fixing it actually means. This is the bit most people skip. Don't.

What you are doing here

You're connecting their problem (no experience) to a consequence they care about (cannot get the job, does not stand out, rejected). They need to feel it, not just know it.

Real example
LinkedIn
I have tried applying but most placements want you to already have experience, it is like a catch 22
Yeah that catch-22 is real. And with that field being competitive - do you feel like not having anything on the CV yet could affect your options when you start applying properly?
I - implication of the gap
Yeah honestly a bit worried about it. Like everyone else will have something and I will not

What just happened

They said "a bit worried." You did not tell them to be worried - you asked a question that helped them connect the dots. Now the problem has stakes. That's when people are genuinely ready to hear a solution.

Important: One strong implication question is enough. You're not trying to make them feel bad - you are helping them see the situation clearly.
Practice
They're in their first year at college, want to go into marketing. They've told you they have no real experience and their LinkedIn is empty.
Which implication question works best?

N - NEED-PAYOFF.
LET THEM SELL IT TO THEMSELVES.

Need-Payoff questions get the other person to describe exactly what would solve their problem. Then you show them that is what Inspiring Me does.

The principle

When someone says "I wish I could find something that gives me real experience and looks good on my CV" - and then you show them Inspiring Me does exactly that - they have basically convinced themselves. You did not push them. They pulled themselves.

Real example
LinkedIn
Yeah honestly a bit worried about it. Like everyone else will have something and I will not
Makes sense. If you could find something that gave you actual practical experience - something that would show up properly on a CV - would that be useful to you right now?
N - need-payoff question
Yeah 100% - something that is actually worth putting on there, not just shadowing
That's exactly what I wanted to talk to you about actually - there is a programme that gives you real field sales experience, a built LinkedIn profile, and a certificate. All of it counts. Worth a quick look?
N → bridge to programme

The bridge line

"That's exactly what I wanted to talk to you about actually" - this is your bridge. You've waited until they have described the solution themselves. Now when you introduce Inspiring Me, it is not a pitch. It's an answer.

Practice
They've said they want something that proves they can communicate and handle pressure. Going into law, knows they need more than just grades.
Which is the best Need-Payoff question?

BUILDING
VALUE.

Once you have introduced the programme, you do not dump all the information. You connect each part directly to what they told you they needed. That's how value is built.

The rule

Every feature you mention must be attached to their specific situation. Feature → their benefit. Not a list - a conversation. Match what the programme offers to what they said they were missing.

Feature → Benefit

  • Field sales experience → "Real experience you can talk to in interviews - not just shadowing"
  • LinkedIn profile build → "By the end you have got a profile that actually gets noticed by recruiters"
  • Certificate → "Proof that goes on your CV - not just something you say, something you can show"
  • Weekend programme → "Fits around school or college - you do not have to drop anything"
  • Online self-development → "Each week you are adding something new to your LinkedIn - you can see it building"
Real example - building value
WhatsApp
Yeah I mean if it is legit I would be interested but I have seen a lot of these things that do not really lead anywhere
That's fair - and I get the scepticism. What makes this different is it is an actual registered CIC. And you said you wanted something that'd actually show up on a CV - the certificate and the LinkedIn build are both tangible. You'd leave with things you can point to.
V - value built from their concern
Ok that does sound better. What's it actually involve day to day?
So weekends you are out doing face-to-face sales - selling the Inspiring Me magazine across London. That's where you get the experience. Then during the week there is a quick online module - that is the self-dev side, keeps the LinkedIn moving. Fits around whatever you have got on.
V - matched to their schedule concern
On earning: If it comes up naturally, say "yes it is a programme where you earn based on what you put in." Say it once, move on. Never make it the headline.
Practice
They've said they want something that actually proves they can communicate, and they are worried about CV gaps going into nursing applications.
You're building value. Which response works best?

THE
CLOSE.

The close is not a pressure moment - it is a natural next step. If you have done SPIN properly, closing feels easy. You're just helping them take an action they already want to take.

When to close

  • They've said the programme sounds good, or asked for more info.
  • They've described exactly what Inspiring Me offers as what they need.
  • Objections have been handled and they are still engaged.
  • They have not said no - they have just not said yes yet.

How to close - typed

Keep it light. One clear next step. The form link is your close. Don't make it feel like a big commitment - frame it as "just taking a look" or "getting your details in."

Close lines that work
  • "If it sounds like it could work, best thing is just fill in the short form - takes 2 minutes: [link]"
  • "Want to get your details in? It's just a quick application, no pressure: [link]"
  • "Worth putting yourself forward - worst case you find out it is not for you. Here's the form: [link]"
  • "Easiest next step is just getting your details in - we can go from there: [link]"

WhatsApp
Ok that actually sounds good. How do I find out more?
Best thing is just to put your details in - short form, takes 2 mins. Once you are in someone can go through everything with you. Here: https://pixel-perfect-remake-63.lovable.app/apply
C - clean close with link
Ok cool ill have a look 👍
Nice one. Let me know if you have any questions before you fill it in 🙌
C - leave the door open

Snapchat

Sick - easiest thing is just to fill in a quick form and we go from there. No commitment, just getting your details in: https://pixel-perfect-remake-63.lovable.app/apply

Yeah ok sending it now

After the close

If they do not fill it in within 24 hours, one light follow-up is fine: "Hey - did you manage to get a look at that form? Happy to answer anything if you are not sure." One follow-up. Then leave it.

Practice
They've said "sounds interesting, I might be up for that." Engaged but not committed yet.
What's the best close?

OBJECTION
HANDLING.

Objections aren't rejections. They're questions in disguise. When someone pushes back, they are still engaged - they just need something answered before they can move forward.

The formula

Acknowledge → Answer → Connect back. Never argue. Never skip the acknowledge. People need to feel heard before they'll hear you.

"It sounds like a scam / too good to be true"

Totally fair - there is a lot of stuff online that looks similar and is not. This is a registered CIC - it is publicly listed. Happy to send you the company number if that helps? The LinkedIn page is live too, you can see real people who've been through it.

"I cannot do Saturdays"

Yeah Saturdays are the main days - though it is worth having a chat because sometimes Sunday can work depending on your area. What's usually going on for you on Saturdays?

"It's too far"

The first session starts from Finsbury Park but the actual sales work is across London - busy stations and town centres. Is it specifically getting to the first meeting point that is the issue?

"I do not have time"

That's the thing - it runs on weekends - Saturdays and Sundays - so it does not touch your school or college week. And the online side is flexible, you do it when it suits you. What does your weekend usually look like?

"What's the catch / how does the pay work"

No catch - it is a programme, so you earn based on what you do. The main value is the experience and certificate honestly - that is what stays with you. But yes, there is earning alongside it.
Remember on pay: Acknowledge it, answer simply, then move back to the value. Never dwell on it.
